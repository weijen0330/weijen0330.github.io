Carnival Cruise Line

A mobile app with a simple design tailored for the Carnival brand that allows the cruise guests to maximize event participation and coordination among families and friends.

The Challenge

This is the second project my partner and I completed in INFO 330. Here are the personas we have to work with:

The app needs to help the users experience the ship's many events and activities without being a menu of directories or a calendaring program. Instead, we are designing an intuitive tool to discover events based on customers' interests, share and collaborate with others in their party, and meet other passengers. The last goal is especially important for the the singles.

Figuring It Out

Like The Walking Dead fansite, we stayed away from the computers during the design phase by creating paper prototypes. Below are results of the first iteration and we separated the functionality into three sections.

When the users first turn on the app, they will be asked to scan the QR code on their wristband ticket. We wanted to incorporate a wearable element to eliminate a large amount of manual typing on the cruise. When the passengers boarded the boat, they should be in vacation mode and the last thing they should worry about would be to provide us with personal information. We will still be able to obtain that information with a survey when they sign up for the cruise at home. The wristband will then be mailed to their house or can be printed at home.

The first section would be the "Notices" section. Our goal here is to feed as many events to the users as possible, which explains the "Happening Now" block. Right below that, there is the "Request" block, which I will elaborate later during the "Party" section. Last but not least, we have an "Announcement" block for any weather warnings.

The "Schedules" will be the second section. The users can add events to their schedule by clicking the "+". Events are organized based on time, with the ones that are happening now at the top because again, we want to our passengers to attend as many events as possible, so if they forget to register, they still have a chance of participating.

For each individual event, the users can check out the location, price, contact the manager, see who in their party is going and invite someone in their party to go with them.

The parents can use this section to sign up events for their kids.

Last, we have the "Party" section. All the members in your party will be automatically synced when the QR code is scanned. This is for contacting members in your party, which is key for families to communicate with their kids. If the users meet someone on the boat that they would like to hangout with, they can add them to the party by scanning the QR code on their wristband, which will be popular among the singles.

This section ties back to the "Request" block in the "Notices" section when let's say a cute girl wants to add me to her party. I will then let her scan my wristband. Then, in my "Request" area, I will see the cute girl's name and she wants to join my party. After I accept the request, she can then invite me to events or message me. If she turns out to be creepy, I can delete her here in this section. We think we hit the sweet point between security and easy to meet new people.

After presenting our work to the class, here were the feedback we received:

  1. There are going to be big events that the passengers do not want to miss, and our app doesn't inform them with that information.
  2. There is no way of cancelling an event. Only attending.
  3. The users might click the delete button easily. We should have some sort of confirmation.

The Solution

Here is our final product as clickable prototype! We were able to execute the majority of our original ideas, but we did make some minor adjustments.

In the "Notices" section, we added the "Don't Miss These Events" part to resolve the first issue. This will inform the customers about popular events that they should definitely participate.

For the "Schedule" section, much of it stayed the same. We addressed the second issue by simply making the "Attend" button into a toggle, so if the user is attending the event but wants to cancel, he or she can just press the button again.

If the users want to see the other people's schedules (in their party only), they can tab on their name icons and the schedules will merge together and be color coded. There is a line through the four names at the bottom. On the left side are the people who are originally in the party, and on the right are the people who are added later through the app.

Last but not least, we kept everything from the "Party" section, but added the confirmation element to address our third issue. If the users accidentally deleted someone from their original party, they can just scan his or her ticket again.
